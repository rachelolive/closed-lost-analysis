What's Driving Losses in 2026

1. Contact Unresponsive/Disengagement: This is the single biggest pattern (examples: Honeywell, Conde Nast, NextEra, P&G, CIBC, Johnson & Johnson, TPG Telecom, Bloomreach, etc). Deals get to demo or discovery stage, then simply go dark.

2. Pricing & Budget Constraints: Grammarly, MSL Group, Multiverse, Appcharge, Brockwell, Turner & Townsend, ING Group, Tata Communications, and others all cited budget cuts or competitive undercutting. Meltwater, Cision, Kantar, and Vuelio are consistently named as cheaper alternatives. The Darktrace loss is a good example — the contact switched to Claude (Anthropic's AI) to generate newsletters at 35p a run, bypassing the need for Signal entirely.

3. Non-Core / Wrong ICP: ADVC, Making Science, Brunswick Advisory, Xylem Tree, Airbnb, SS&C, SHL. Several of these came in through Marketing/Content and Marketing/Request a Demo channels, which is a direct flag for marketing's lead qualification process.

4. Org Change & Contact Departure: Take-Two, Wolters Kluwer, New York Mets, Western Union, Tyson Foods, both Deloitte South Africa entries — contacts leaving or restructures killing momentum. Hard to prevent, but symptomatic of single-threaded deals.

5. Product/Content Gaps (~8%): Social listening gaps: Unilever (lost to Polecat, ~£280k ARR) and Decathlon Strategy both explicitly cited social firehose coverage as the dealbreaker. York Park and Brockwell both lost explicitly because Signal doesn't have a NewsCorp partnership. BCG Reg Risk and TCW Group lost because Signal couldn't provide full text for WSJ, Barron's and top publications. Methodology mismatch: Chevron, National Info Centre KSA, Syneos Health — Signal's approach didn't fit their risk frameworks.

6. Not in Buying Cycle/Timing: SC Johnson, FTI, Sun Life, Ferring, CVS Health, Weber Shandwick. These are timing losses, not rejection losses.

What This Means for Us
  • Lead quality is a problem: A significant number of non-core use cases (no PR function, wrong buying intent) are entering the funnel through marketing channels (particularly Request a Demo and Content/Webinar inbounds).
  • There is a nurture gap: "Contact Unresponsive" is the #1 loss reason. Many of these deals had genuine interest but fell into a black hole after the first demo. Marketing needs a structured post-demo nurture program — case studies, ROI content, and automated sequences.
  • Competitive messaging needs sharpening: Meltwater, Cision, Kantar, and Vuelio are winning on price. The Darktrace deal is a red flag: AI tools like Claude are now being used to solve the exact newsletter/monitoring use case Signal targets — at near-zero cost. This is an emerging competitive threat.
  • Re-engagement lists: Several accounts explicitly said "come back in Q2/Q3" or "revisit when budget opens": Jacobs Engineering, Sun Life, Appcharge, Decathlon Employer Branding, Ferring, Chevron, Brookfield, FTI. They're warm — they just need the right moment.
  • Timing problem: Many Q1 losses cite budget freezes and restructures. This is likely a macro Q1 pattern. Marketing should examine whether outreach cadences are calibrated to when buyers actually evaluate tools — for enterprise, that's typically Q3/Q4.
